As a member of SNU rocket team HANARO, I participated in 2019 Spaceport America Cup, 10K feet with Commercial of the Shelf solid motor.  
I was part of avionics system development. Our system consists of Raspberry Pi as main MCU and Arduino as sensor controller.   
Arduino aquires datas from IMU and barometer, and send them to Raspberry Pi via USB serial communication.   
Raspberry Pi analyses datas from Arduino and Iridum GPS. It commucicates with ground station via Wi-Fi or Iridium satellite communication. Also, it controlls parachuete deploy module.   

![Assembly](/assets/images/rocket assembly.PNG){: width="48%" height="48%"}
![Avionics](/assets/images/avionics.PNG){: width="48%" height="48%"}

I mainly worked for sensor data processing. I worked on codes for sensor data acquisition from arduino and its communication with Raspberry Pi.   
In Raspberry Pi, I implemented EKF for sensor fusion algorithm. This processed position estimation is used for parachute deployment condition check.  
To prevent rocket from landing too far away from launch site, 2 parahchute system must be implemented.
If Raspberry Pi thinks the rocket's altitude is decreasing, it deploys drogue parachute. After descending, in the 500m altitude, the avionics system deploys main parachute.

![Mission_overview](/assets/images/mission_concept.PNG){: width="80%" height="80%"}

Our recovery system is actuated with compressed air. Due to safety regulations in Korea, we could not use black powder as American teams. Therefore, if parachute signal is given, Raspberry Pi gives current to latch valve and release air deploys parachutes

![Recovery_system](/assets/images/recovery.PNG){: width="80%" height="80%"}

We successfully deployed parachute and retrieved rocket with this avionics module.
